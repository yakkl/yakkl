# Yakkl overview

Yakkl is a powerful, open source group chat application that combines the
immediacy of real-time chat with the productivity benefits of threaded
conversations. Yakkl is used by open source projects, Fortune 500 companies,
large standards bodies, and others who need a real-time chat system that
allows users to easily process hundreds or thousands of messages a day. With
over 500 contributors merging over 500 commits a month, Yakkl is also the
largest and fastest growing open source group chat project.

## Getting started

Click on the appropriate link below. If nothing seems to apply,
join us on the
[Yakkl community server](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing/chat-yakkl-org.html)
and tell us what's up!

You might be interested in:

* **Contributing code**. Check out our
  [guide for new contributors](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/overview/contributing.html)
  to get started. Yakkl prides itself on maintaining a clean and
  well-tested codebase, and a stock of hundreds of
  [beginner-friendly issues][beginner-friendly].

* **Contributing non-code**.
  [Report an issue](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/overview/contributing.html#reporting-issue),
  [translate](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/translating/translating.html) Yakkl
  into your language,
  [write](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/overview/contributing.html#yakkl-outreach)
  for the Yakkl blog, or
  [give us feedback](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/overview/contributing.html#user-feedback). We
  would love to hear from you, even if you're just trying the product out.

* **Supporting Yakkl**. Advocate for your organization to use Yakkl, write a
  review in the mobile app stores, or
  [upvote Yakkl](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/overview/contributing.html#yakkl-outreach) on
  product comparison sites.

* **Checking Yakkl out**. The best way to see Yakkl in action is to drop by
  the
  [Yakkl community server](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/contributing/chat-yakkl-org.html). We
  also recommend reading Yakkl for
  [open source](https://yakkl.com/for/open-source/), Yakkl for
  [companies](https://yakkl.com/for/companies/), or Yakkl for
  [working groups and part time communities](https://yakkl.com/for/working-groups-and-communities/).

* **Running a Yakkl server**. Setting up a server takes just a couple
  of minutes. Yakkl runs on Ubuntu 18.04 Bionic, Ubuntu 16.04 Xenial,
  and Debian 9 Stretch. The installation process is
  [documented here](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/stable/production/install.html).
  Commercial support is available; see <https://yakkl.com/plans>
  for details.

* **Using Yakkl without setting up a server**. <https://yakkl.com> offers
  free and commercial hosting.

* **Applying for a Yakkl internship**. Yakkl runs internship programs with
  [Outreachy](https://www.outreachy.org/),
  [Google Summer of Code](https://developers.google.com/open-source/gsoc/),
  and the
  [MIT Externship program](https://alum.mit.edu/students/NetworkwithAlumni/ExternshipProgram). Yakkl
  also participates in
  [Google Code-In](https://developers.google.com/open-source/gci/). More
  information is available
  [here](https://yakkl.readthedocs.io/en/latest/overview/contributing.html#internship-programs).

You may also be interested in reading our [blog](http://blog.yakkl.org/) or
following us on [twitter](https://twitter.com/yakkl).
